===============================================
1) DIABETES MELLITUS TYPE 2
===============================================
Overview:
Diabetes Mellitus Type 2 is a chronic metabolic disorder where the body becomes resistant to insulin or does not produce enough insulin.

Symptoms:
• Frequent urination
• Excessive thirst
• Fatigue
• Blurred vision
• Slow wound healing

Risk Factors:
• Obesity
• Family history
• Sedentary lifestyle
• Poor diet

Diagnosis:
• Fasting blood glucose
• Oral glucose tolerance test
• HbA1c test

Treatment:
• Lifestyle modification
• Metformin
• Insulin therapy in advanced cases

Complications:
• Neuropathy
• Kidney disease
• Eye damage (retinopathy)


===============================================
2) HYPERTENSION
===============================================
Overview:
Hypertension is chronically elevated blood pressure, usually above 140/90 mmHg.

Symptoms:
• Headache
• Dizziness
• Vision problems
• Nosebleeds (rare)

Risk Factors:
• High salt intake
• Stress
• Smoking
• Obesity

Diagnosis:
• Blood pressure measurement
• ABPM (Ambulatory BP Monitoring)

Treatment:
• ACE inhibitors
• Beta-blockers
• Lifestyle changes


===============================================
3) CORONARY ARTERY DISEASE (HEART DISEASE)
===============================================
Overview:
Heart disease occurs when coronary arteries become blocked or narrowed due to plaque buildup.

Symptoms:
• Chest pain (angina)
• Shortness of breath
• Fatigue
• Palpitations

Risk Factors:
• High cholesterol
• Hypertension
• Smoking
• Diabetes

Diagnosis:
• ECG
• Echocardiogram
• Coronary angiography

Treatment:
• Antiplatelets (Aspirin)
• Statins
• Angioplasty or bypass surgery in severe cases


===============================================
4) PNEUMONIA
===============================================
Overview:
Pneumonia is an infection that inflames the air sacs in the lungs.

Symptoms:
• Fever
• Productive cough
• Chest pain
• Difficulty breathing

Risk Factors:
• Smoking
• Chronic lung disease
• Low immunity

Diagnosis:
• Chest X-ray
• CBC blood test

Treatment:
• Antibiotics
• Oxygen support
• Rest and hydration


===============================================
5) CHRONIC KIDNEY DISEASE (CKD)
===============================================
Overview:
CKD is gradual loss of kidney function lasting more than 3 months.

Symptoms:
• Swollen feet
• Fatigue
• Nausea
• Reduced urine output

Risk Factors:
• Diabetes
• Hypertension
• Family history

Diagnosis:
• eGFR test
• Urine albumin

Treatment:
• BP control medication
• Dialysis in advanced stages
• Kidney transplant (end stage)
